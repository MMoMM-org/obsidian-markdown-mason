# when will sakura be in tokyo next year

In Tokyo next year (2026), sakura are forecast to start blooming around March 19–20 and reach their best viewing period roughly March 26–April 4.[^1_1][^1_2][^1_3][^1_4][^1_5][^1_6]

## Key dates for Tokyo 2026

- First blossoms (kaika): Around March 19–20 in central Tokyo.[^1_2][^1_7][^1_4][^1_5][^1_6][^1_1]
- Full bloom (mankai): Around March 26–28.[^1_8][^1_3][^1_4][^1_6][^1_1][^1_2]
- “Best viewing” window: Commonly given as about March 28–April 4 for Tokyo.[^1_3][^1_9]

So if you aim your trip for roughly March 27–April 2, you maximize the probability of catching peak hanami in the city, assuming a typical weather year.[^1_4][^1_10][^1_2][^1_3]

## Practical planning tip

Forecasts can still shift by several days due to warm/cold spells, so if you are locking in flights early, consider a 7–10 day window centered on March 27 to hedge against variation.[^1_11][^1_10][^1_2][^1_3]

# when will it be in Sapporo?

In Sapporo in 2026, sakura are expected roughly from late April, with peak around the last week of April.[^2_1][^2_2][^2_3][^2_4][^2_5][^2_6][^2_7][^2_8]

## Key dates for Sapporo 2026

- First blossoms: Forecasts cluster around April 18–25 for first bloom.[^2_1][^2_2][^2_5][^2_6][^2_7][^2_8]
- Full bloom: Around April 24–May 1, depending on the forecaster.[^2_2][^2_3][^2_6][^2_7][^2_8][^2_1]
- Best viewing window: A safe bet is roughly April 24–30 for Sapporo city parks such as Maruyama Park and Hokkaido Shrine.[^2_3][^2_4][^2_8]

So if you’re aligning this with Tokyo, think Tokyo around March 27–April 2, then Sapporo about three to four weeks later, around April 24–30.[^2_4][^2_8][^2_9]
