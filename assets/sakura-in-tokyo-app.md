## Question

When will sakura be in Tokyo next year?

## Answer

In Tokyo next year (2026), sakura are forecast to start blooming around March 19–20 and reach their best viewing period roughly March 26–April 4.[1][2][3][4][5][6]

## Key dates for Tokyo 2026

- First blossoms (kaika): Around March 19–20 in central Tokyo.[2][4][5][6][7][1]
- Full bloom (mankai): Around March 26–28.[3][4][6][8][1][2]
- “Best viewing” window: Commonly given as about March 28–April 4 for Tokyo.[9][3]

So if you aim your trip for roughly March 27–April 2, you maximize the probability of catching peak hanami in the city, assuming a typical weather year.[4][10][2][3]

## Practical planning tip

Forecasts can still shift by several days due to warm/cold spells, so if you are locking in flights early, consider a 7–10 day window centered on March 27 to hedge against variation.[10][11][2][3]

## Question

When will it be in Sapporo?

## Answer

In Sapporo in 2026, sakura are expected roughly from late April, with peak around the last week of April.[1][2][3][4][5][6][7][8]

## Key dates for Sapporo 2026

- First blossoms: Forecasts cluster around April 18–25 for first bloom.[1][2][5][6][7][8]
- Full bloom: Around April 24–May 1, depending on the forecaster.[2][3][6][7][8][1]
- Best viewing window: A safe bet is roughly April 24–30 for Sapporo city parks such as Maruyama Park and Hokkaido Shrine.[3][4][8]

So if you’re aligning this with Tokyo, think Tokyo around March 27–April 2, then Sapporo about three to four weeks later, around April 24–30.[4][8][9]
